Air Jordan 3 “Cement Grey”

CT8532-106
Betrayal arc Learning moment Not for my feet

The context

When I bought these, I was about five months into working at Champs and still very much not a shoe guy. I didn’t love Jordan 3s, didn’t really understand the hype, and honestly wasn’t even sold on the silhouette.

My full-time associate at the time, Jet, convinced me to buy them. He told me they were his favourite Jordan.

The mistake

I wore them to work the very next day.

Two hours into my shift, my feet were absolutely killing me. I went back to Jet and said, “Man, these are brutal.”

Without missing a beat, he replied: “I said they were my favourite. I never said they were comfortable.”

I’ll never forget that moment. Utter betrayal.

What they taught me

As much as I don’t love wearing them, these shoes were a real lesson early on: hype, history, and personal comfort are three completely different things.

Just because a shoe is iconic (and the Jordan 3 absolutely is) doesn’t mean it’s right for everyone. Especially not my feet.

Why they’re still here

Even though I genuinely don’t like wearing them, I keep them because of what they represent. They’re a snapshot of being new, trusting other people’s opinions, and learning quickly.

Plus… the story is too good not to keep.

Quick notes

Standout detail: Cement grey accents and elephant print

Comfort level: Personally offensive

Personal meaning: A lesson I’ll never forget
